British Virgin Islands requirements at a glance

BVI VASP registration requires BVI BC, FSC registration, AML program, and operational substance (lighter than EU but real).

  • BVI Business Company: BVI BC (Business Company) formation. Standard offshore vehicle. No minimum capital.
  • BVI registered agent: Mandatory licensed BVI registered agent (typically a BVI law firm or trust company).
  • Operational financial resources: FSC requires 'sufficient financial resources' — class-dependent, but no fixed minimum capital like MiCA.
  • Fit-and-proper key personnel: Directors and key personnel undergo BVI FSC fit-and-proper review.
  • AML program: Comprehensive program aligned with BVI Anti-Money Laundering Regulations + FATF guidance.
  • IT and security framework: Documented controls, custody segregation (Class 1/2), business continuity.
  • BOSS UBO disclosure: BVI Beneficial Ownership Secure Search (BOSS) register disclosure of UBOs ≥10%.
  • Annual filings + FSC fees: Annual BC renewal, registered agent fee, FSC supervisory fee.

Glossary: key terms for British Virgin Islands crypto setup

If you're new to crypto licensing in British Virgin Islands, these are the terms you'll encounter most often.

VASP Act 2022
BVI Virtual Asset Service Providers Act (2022) — created the dedicated VASP registration framework. Effective February 2023.
BVI FSC
BVI Financial Services Commission — competent authority for VASP registration and supervision.
BVI BC
BVI Business Company — standard offshore corporate vehicle. Used by 400,000+ companies globally. No corporate tax.
Registered Agent
Mandatory licensed BVI representative for any BVI BC. Typically a BVI law firm or trust company. Holds corporate book and handles annual filings.
BOSS Register
Beneficial Ownership Secure Search system — BVI's UBO register (not public; accessible to authorities).
VASP Categories (BVI)
Class 1 Custody, Class 2 Exchange, Class 3 Issuance. Classes can be combined; capital requirements vary.
FATF Compliance
BVI is FATF-aligned with comprehensive AML framework. BVI VASP entities pass bank KYC more easily than purely offshore entities.
Economic Substance Act
BVI requires economic substance for certain activities. VASPs typically satisfy substance via their licensed operations.

British Virgin Islands crypto license — frequently asked questions

The most common questions from founders considering British Virgin Islands for their crypto company in 2026.

How much does a BVI VASP license cost in 2026?

Total setup runs $30,000–$70,000 in year one all-in. BVI FSC fee $10K–$25K (class-dependent), $10K–$25K AML program, $10K–$30K legal counsel, BVI BC formation $3K–$8K. No minimum paid-up capital required (operational financial resources sufficient).

What are the BVI VASP classes?

Three classes under VASP Act 2022: Class 1 Custody ($10K FSC fee — wallet, custody platforms), Class 2 Exchange ($20K FSC fee — crypto exchanges, OTC, brokers), Class 3 Issuance ($25K FSC fee — token issuance, ICO services, primary markets). Classes can be combined; combined fees apply.

BVI VASP vs Panama crypto — which is better?

BVI has a structured VASP framework (VASP Act 2022) with FSC supervision — more credible than Panama's no-specific-license approach. BVI registration carries stronger bank-acceptance signal. Panama is cheaper ($15K–$45K vs $30K–$70K), faster (6–12 weeks vs 3–6 months), and has territorial tax (0% on foreign-source income, similar to BVI's 0% offshore). Choose BVI for institutional/credible offshore; Panama for cost-optimized offshore.

Does BVI VASP grant EU or US passport?

No. BVI VASP is offshore — no EU MiCA passport, no US licensing. EU customers trigger MiCA obligations (need separate Lithuania CASP). US customers trigger FinCEN MSB. Most BVI VASP setups target Latin America, Asia, MENA, and HNW global clients rather than EU/US retail.

How long does BVI VASP registration take?

Total timeline is 3–6 months: BVI BC formation 2 weeks, VASP application preparation 6–10 weeks, FSC review 8–16 weeks. Banking onboarding runs in parallel from week 4. BVI's framework is well-established, so timelines are predictable.

Is BVI on any FATF grey list?

No. BVI is not on the FATF grey or black list. BVI has comprehensive AML legislation aligned with FATF recommendations. BVI VASP entities pass bank KYC more easily than entities from grey-list jurisdictions.
